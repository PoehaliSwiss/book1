## Introduction

Adjective declension is one of the most challenging aspects of German grammar for learners. In German, adjectives change their endings based on three factors:
1. **Gender** of the noun (masculine, feminine, neuter, or plural)
2. **Case** (nominative, accusative, dative, genitive)
3. **Type of article** (definite, indefinite, or no article)

## The Three Declension Patterns

### 1. Weak Declension (after definite articles: der, die, das, die)

Used when the noun has a definite article (der/die/das) or a demonstrative pronoun (dieser/jener).

## Complete Declension Tables

### Weak Declension (after definite article)

| Case | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
|------|-----------|----------|--------|--------|
| Nominative | der alt**e** Mann | die alt**e** Frau | das alt**e** Haus | die alt**en** Häuser |
| Accusative | den alt**en** Mann | die alt**e** Frau | das alt**e** Haus | die alt**en** Häuser |
| Dative | dem alt**en** Mann | der alt**en** Frau | dem alt**en** Haus | den alt**en** Häusern |
| Genitive | des alt**en** Mannes | der alt**en** Frau | des alt**en** Hauses | der alt**en** Häuser |

### Mixed Declension (after indefinite article)

| Case | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
|------|-----------|----------|--------|--------|
| Nominative | ein alt**er** Mann | eine alt**e** Frau | ein alt**es** Haus | keine alt**en** Häuser |
| Accusative | einen alt**en** Mann | eine alt**e** Frau | ein alt**es** Haus | keine alt**en** Häuser |
| Dative | einem alt**en** Mann | einer alt**en** Frau | einem alt**en** Haus | keinen alt**en** Häusern |
| Genitive | eines alt**en** Mannes | einer alt**en** Frau | eines alt**en** Hauses | keiner alt**en** Häuser |

### Strong Declension (no article)

| Case | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
|------|-----------|----------|--------|--------|
| Nominative | alt**er** Mann | alt**e** Frau | alt**es** Haus | alt**e** Häuser |
| Accusative | alt**en** Mann | alt**e** Frau | alt**es** Haus | alt**e** Häuser |
| Dative | alt**em** Mann | alt**er** Frau | alt**em** Haus | alt**en** Häusern |
| Genitive | alt**en** Mannes | alt**er** Frau | alt**en** Hauses | alt**er** Häuser |

## Special Cases and Exceptions

### Adjectives Ending in -el, -er, -en
These adjectives often drop the -e- before adding endings:
- dunkel → ein dunkl**er** Raum (not dunkeler)
- teuer → der teur**e** Wein (not teuerer)

### Irregular Adjectives
Some common irregular forms:
- hoch → hoher (not hocher)
- nah → naher (not naher)

## Summary

Remember these key points:
1. **Weak declension**: Mostly -e or -en endings
2. **Mixed declension**: Mix of strong and weak endings
3. **Strong declension**: Endings similar to definite articles
4. Always consider: Gender + Case + Article type

Practice regularly with different nouns and cases to internalize the patterns!
